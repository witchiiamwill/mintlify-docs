## FOLLOWERS OF SET WL QUESTIONNAIRE\
\
### 1) GENERAL QUESTIONS\
- Why do you want to play a Follower of Set?\
- What interests you beyond “seduction” or “evil cultist” tropes?\
\
### 2) VALUES AND MINDSET\
\*Setites see themselves as liberators, not just corrupters.\*\
- What does “corruption” mean to your character? Is it destruction, enlightenment, or something else?\
\
\*Setite faith is not monolithic (traditional cult of Set, syncretic beliefs, modern reinterpretations).\*\
- Which “version” of Setite belief does your character follow?\
- How does this affect their behavior and goals?\
- In your character’s view, who should be called a true Setite?\
Explain your reasoning.\
\
\*Setites often operate through temples, cults, and influence networks.\*\
- What role does your character play in this structure?\
- Priest, corrupter, recruiter, guardian of lore, or something else?\
- How does your character “corrupt” others?\
- Subtle manipulation, offering freedom, exploiting weakness, or something more ideological?\
- How does your character justify the harm they cause?\
- Do they see themselves as evil, necessary, or righteous?\
\
### 3) PICK AND WRITE _TWO_ OF THE THREE FOLLOWING IC SCENES\
\*You encounter someone vulnerable but not yet corrupted.\*\
- They trust you.\
- You could genuinely help them—or guide them into your web.\
What do you do, and how do you present your choice to them?\
\
\*Another Setite operates in a way you consider heretical or misguided.\*\
- Their methods are effective but contradict your beliefs.\
Do you confront, convert, undermine, or cooperate with them—and why?\
\
\*Someone you have been cultivating fully embraces their “freedom”… and becomes destructive, endangering your operations and others.\*\
- They claim you showed them the truth.\
Write a short response (dialogue or internal monologue):\
 Do you take responsibility, correct them, eliminate them, or let them continue—and how do you justify it within your faith?
