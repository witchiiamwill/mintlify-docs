## BANU HAQIM WL QUESTIONNAIRE\
### 1) GENERAL QUESTIONS\
- Why do you want to play a Banu Haqim?\
- What aspect interests you most: judge, warrior, or scholar?\
\
### 2) VALUES AND MINDSET\
- _Banu Haqim see themselves as judges, not just killers._\
What does “justice” mean to your character ? Who gives them the right to enforce it?\
- _The clan is divided into Warriors, Viziers, and Sorcerers._\
Which caste does your character belong to, and how do they view the other two?\
Include at least one point of tension.\
- What are the Laws of Haqim to your character: sacred truth, guideline, or political tool?\
Name one law they follow strictly and one they struggle with.\
- _Banu Haqim must balance judgment with control._\
Describe a situation where your character wanted to punish someone - but chose not to. Why?\
- _Most Banu Haqim operate with strong codes or beliefs._\
When your character’s personal code conflicts with practical survival, which wins? Give an example.\
- _Banu Haqim are feared assassins - deadly and precise._\
Describe how your character decides someone deserves death. What proof or threshold do they require?\
\
### 3) PICK AND WRITE _TWO_ OF THE THREE FOLLOWING IC SCENES\
\
- You are asked to judge a Kindred accused of diablerie.\
\*Evidence is unclear. Influential figures want a guilty verdict.\*\
How does your character proceed? What matters most in their decision?\
\
- A member of another caste breaks one of the Laws of Haqim - but their actions benefited the clan.\
\*Do you expose them, punish them, or protect them…?\*\
\
- You discover that your sire has violated the Laws of Haqim.\
\*Reporting them will destroy your lineage and standing.\
Ignoring it makes you complicit.\*\
Write a short response (dialogue or internal monologue) showing your decision, and how your character justifies it.
