## GIOVANNI WL QUESTIONNAIRE\
### 1) GENERAL QUESTIONS\
- Why do you want to play a Giovanni?\
- What interests you beyond “necromancer” or “mafia vampire”?\
\
### 2) VALUES AND MINDSET\
\*The Giovanni are first and foremost a family.\*\
 - What does “family” mean to your character?\
Loyalty, obligation, tool for power, or something else?\
\
\*The Giovanni are not perfectly unified and include internal branches and aligned families.\*\
- How does your character fit within the clan’s internal structure?\
Core Giovanni lineage?\
A subordinate/associated family?\
Ambitious outsider trying to rise?\
Include at least one internal tension or rivalry that affects them.\
\
\*The Giovanni Embrace is selective and often transactional.\*\
- Why was your character Embraced?\
What value did they bring to the family—and what is now expected of them?\
\
\*Giovanni rely heavily on mortal assets.\*\
- How does your character view and treat ghouls or revenants?\
 Assets, family, tools, or something in between?\
\
\*Necromancy is central to the clan.\*\
- How does your character view necromancy?\
Sacred duty?\
Tool for power?\
Means to understand/control death?\
\
- What is their personal relationship to the dead?\
- How does your character justify manipulating, binding, or exploiting spirits?\
- Do they see the dead as people, resources, or something else?\
\
### 3) PICK AND WRITE _TWO_ OF THE THREE FOLLOWING IC SCENES\
\*A mortal relative has become a liability to the family’s operations.\*\
\*They trust you completely.\*\
\*The family expects you to handle it.\*\
- What do you do, and how do you justify it?\
\
\*Another Giovanni (or allied family member) is undermining you politically.\*\
\*Open conflict would harm the family’s interests.\*\
- How do you deal with them while preserving—or appearing to preserve—family unity?\
\
\*You bind a spirit that resists and pleads for release, revealing information that could be valuable.\*\
\*Keeping it bound gives you power.\*\
\*Releasing it costs you that advantage.\*\
- Write a short response (dialogue or internal monologue):\
 - What do you choose, and how do you justify your treatment of the dead?
